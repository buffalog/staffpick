# Service Area & Zones

Your service area decides which cases are physically reachable for you. It's set in **Step 3** of your profile and has two parts: a **radius** and an optional **drawn zone**.

## How radius works

You set two distances from your base location:

- **Preferred radius** — the distance you'd ideally stay within. Cases inside it score best.
- **Maximum radius** — the furthest you'll go. Cases beyond it aren't offered to you at all.

Radius is the quick way to describe your area: two concentric circles around your home/base. A case has to fall inside your maximum radius to even be matched to you, and the closer it is, the higher you rank for it — distance is the single biggest factor in matching.

## Drawing a service zone

A circle isn't always the right shape — maybe you'll cross a bridge but not a county line, or you avoid a stretch of highway. The **service zone** lets you draw the exact area you cover on a map:

1. On the Step 3 map, use the polygon tool to click out the boundary of your area.
2. Close the shape. You can edit or redraw it any time.
3. StaffPick stores the polygon (and a bounding box for fast filtering).

## How it affects matching

The radius and the zone work **together**: a case is reachable if it's within your radius and, when you've drawn one, inside your zone. Drawing a zone narrows a too-generous circle down to where you'll really go — which means the cases you're offered are ones you'll actually want to accept.

## Tips

- If you're getting offered cases that are technically in range but impractical, draw a zone to tighten it.
- Leave the zone empty if a simple radius already describes your area — the radius alone is enough.
- Update your area when you move or change how far you'll travel; it takes effect on the next match.
